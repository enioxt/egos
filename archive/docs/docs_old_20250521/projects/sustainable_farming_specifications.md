# Sustainable Farming Specifications in Patos de Minas

## 1. Project Overview

The Sustainable Farming project is located in Patos de Minas, MG, with a total area of 50,846.05m² (coordinates: -18.521160, -46.502563). The project aims to create a residential space that integrates principles of sustainability, privacy, and community living, using the golden ratio to optimize the aesthetics and functionality of the layout.

### 1.1 Concept and Characteristics

The project stands out for its architecture of interconnected groves, spaces for community events, and focus on integration with nature. Main features:

- System of interconnected groves (12% of total area)
- Central gazebo for community events of up to 80 people
- Light earthmoving measures following the natural slope
- Utilization of existing vegetation and strategic expansion
- Attractiveness for birds and local fauna with feeders and nests

### 1.2 Area Distribution (According to Complementary Law No. 508/2015)

- **Total Area:** 50,846.05m²
- **Roads (18%):** 9,150m² (Total width of 9m: 6m bed + 1.5m sidewalk each side)
- **Green Areas (12%):** 6,102m² (Central grove, pocket groves, trails)
- **Institutional Areas (5%):** Attached to the central grove in strategic location
- **Total Public Areas (35%):** 17,796m²
- **Private Lots (65%):** 33,050m² (25 lots with average size of 1,322m²)

## 2. Lot Specifications

### 2.1 Lot Dimensions

- **Average Size:** 1,322m²
- **Minimum Size:** 1,000m²
- **Maximum Size:** 1,800m²
- **Minimum Frontage:** 20m
- **Average Depth:** 55-65m

### 2.2 Lot Layout

- Organic layout following the terrain's natural contours
- Positioning optimized for privacy and views
- Strategic location of lots in relation to green areas
- Lots with direct access to pocket groves
- Positioning that maximizes natural ventilation and lighting

## 3. Road System

### 3.1 Main Access

- **Width:** 9m total (6m roadbed + 1.5m sidewalk on each side)
- **Location:** Southeast of the property
- **Connection:** Municipal road connecting to BR-365

### 3.2 Internal Roads

- **Primary Roads:** 9m wide (6m roadbed + 1.5m sidewalk on each side)
- **Curves:** Minimum radius of 9m
- **Maximum Slope:** 10%
- **Road Surface:** Simple surface treatment (SST)
- **Sidewalks:** Permeable interlocking pavers

### 3.3 Pedestrian Circulation

- **Sidewalks:** 1.5m wide on both sides of all roads
- **Trails:** 1.2m wide connecting the groves
- **Accessibility:** Ramps at all intersections and crossings

## 4. Green Areas

### 4.1 Central Grove

- **Area:** 3,500m²
- **Location:** Center of the property
- **Features:**
  - Community gazebo (80-person capacity)
  - Fruit trees and native species
  - Bird and butterfly garden
  - Meditation space
  - Rainwater harvesting system

### 4.2 Pocket Groves

- **Quantity:** 5 pocket groves
- **Average Size:** 500m² each
- **Distribution:** Strategically positioned throughout the property
- **Features:**
  - Resting areas with benches
  - Native vegetation
  - Bird feeders and nests
  - Small clearings for contemplation

### 4.3 Ecological Corridors

- **Width:** 3-5m
- **Function:** Connect all groves to create a continuous green network
- **Vegetation:** Dense shrubs and small trees to facilitate fauna movement

## 5. Community Areas

### 5.1 Central Gazebo

- **Area:** 150m²
- **Capacity:** 80 people
- **Features:**
  - Open design with natural ventilation
  - Sustainable construction materials
  - Solar panels for lighting
  - Rainwater harvesting system
  - Outdoor kitchen area
  - Accessible restrooms

### 5.2 Institutional Area

- **Total Area:** 2,544m²
- **Location:** Adjacent to the central grove
- **Possible Uses:**
  - Environmental education center
  - Community garden
  - Small sports court
  - Administrative office

## 6. Sustainability Features

### 6.1 Water Management

- Rainwater harvesting systems in community areas
- Permeable surfaces to maximize infiltration
- Bioswales along roads for natural drainage
- Retention ponds in strategic locations
- Preservation of natural drainage patterns

### 6.2 Vegetation

- Preservation of 85% of existing native trees
- Planting of additional 500+ native trees
- Use of native species in all landscaping
- Creation of fruit tree corridors
- Prohibition of invasive exotic species

### 6.3 Energy Efficiency

- Solar-powered LED lighting in public areas
- Low-impact lighting design to minimize light pollution
- Solar panels on community buildings
- Energy-efficient design guidelines for residences

## 7. Construction Guidelines

### 7.1 Setbacks

- **Front:** 5m minimum
- **Sides:** 3m minimum
- **Rear:** 5m minimum
- **Maximum Building Height:** 9m (2 stories)
- **Maximum Lot Coverage:** 50%
- **Minimum Permeable Area:** 30% of lot area

### 7.2 Architectural Guidelines

- Harmonious integration with the landscape
- Use of sustainable materials encouraged
- Earth tones and natural finishes
- Green roofs and walls encouraged
- Rainwater harvesting systems recommended
- Solar energy systems encouraged

### 7.3 Fencing and Walls

- **Maximum Height:** 2.2m
- **Front Fencing:** Transparent or living fences only (hedges)
- **Side and Rear:** Solid walls permitted with minimum 30% vegetation coverage
- **Materials:** Natural materials encouraged (stone, wood, bamboo)
- **Prohibition:** Barbed wire, electric fencing, or sharp elements

## 8. Legal Framework

### 8.1 Applicable Legislation

- Municipal Law No. 508/2015 (Urban Parceling)
- Municipal Environmental Code
- State Environmental Legislation (COPAM/CERH)
- Federal Law No. 6.766/1979 (Urban Land Parceling)
- Federal Law No. 12.651/2012 (Forest Code)

### 8.2 Required Approvals

- Municipal Urban Planning Department
- Municipal Environmental Department
- State Environmental Agency (SUPRAM)
- Fire Department
- Utility Companies (water, electricity)

### 8.3 Registration and Documentation

- Property registry at the Real Estate Registry Office
- Individual lot registrations
- Condominium convention and internal regulations
- Environmental licenses and permits
- Technical responsibility terms (ART/RRT)

## 9. Infrastructure Specifications

### 9.1 Water Supply System

- Connection to municipal water supply
- Individual water meters for each lot
- Backup community well with treatment system
- Water storage tanks with 2-day autonomy
- Pressure regulation system

### 9.2 Sewage System

- Individual septic tanks with anaerobic filter
- Periodic maintenance program
- Future connection to municipal system when available
- Monitoring of effluent quality

### 9.3 Solid Waste Management

- Selective collection points
- Composting area for organic waste
- Recycling program
- Educational campaigns for waste reduction
- Proper disposal of construction waste

### 9.4 Electrical System

- Underground distribution network
- Connection to electricity utility (CEMIG)
- LED public lighting with photocells
- Low and directed light points (to reduce light pollution)
- Transformation and distribution according to utility standards

### 9.5 Stormwater Drainage System

- Natural drainage following contour lines
- Infiltration ditches at strategic points
- Rainwater harvesting at the central gazebo
- Sustainable drainage system following the terrain slope

### 9.6 Paving and Accessibility

- Paving of road beds (6m) with simple surface treatment (SST)
- Concrete curb and gutter
- Sidewalks (1.5m on each side) in permeable interlocking pavers or similar
- Accessibility ramps at corners and crossings
- Tactile signage in strategic areas

### 9.7 Sustainability Differentials

- Functional landscaping with native species from the Minas Gerais cerrado
- Integrated system of feeders, drinking fountains, and nests for birds
- Low-impact lighting to preserve nocturnal fauna habits
- Sustainable, low-maintenance construction materials
- Preservation of ecological corridors between groves
